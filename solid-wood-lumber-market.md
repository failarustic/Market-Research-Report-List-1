<p><p>The solid wood lumber market is primarily divided into two types: softwood and hardwood lumber. Softwood lumber, sourced from coniferous trees, is generally lighter, more versatile, and often used in construction, framing, and furniture. It tends to be more affordable and readily available. In contrast, hardwood lumber comes from deciduous trees and is denser, offering enhanced durability and aesthetic appeal, making it ideal for high-end furniture, cabinetry, and flooring. Each type caters to different consumer needs and applications in various industries.</p></p>
