<p><ul><li>SOP</li><li>SIP</li><li>DIP</li><li>QFN</li><li>QFP</li><li>SOIC</li><li>Others</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The stamping leadframes market includes various packaging types essential for semiconductor devices. SOP (Small Outline Package) features a compact design for surface mounting. SIP (Single Inline Package) provides single-row configurations for efficient space utilization. DIP (Dual Inline Package) has two parallel rows, ideal for through-hole mounting. QFN (Quad Flat No-lead) offers compact, low-profile designs with exposed pads for improved thermal performance. QFP (Quad Flat Package) is used for high-density applications, while SOIC (Small Outline Integrated Circuit) balances size and pin count. Other types encompass various innovations in semiconductor packaging.</p></p>
