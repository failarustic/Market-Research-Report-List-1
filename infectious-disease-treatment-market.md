<p><p>The infectious disease treatment market comprises three main types: antibacterial, antifungal, and antiviral drugs. Antibacterial drugs target bacterial infections and are widely used due to rising antibiotic resistance. Antifungal drugs combat fungal infections, which are increasingly prevalent in immunocompromised patients. Antiviral drugs are designed to treat viral infections, including HIV, hepatitis, and influenza, leveraging advancements in biotechnology. Together, these markets are crucial for addressing the global burden of infectious diseases and developing effective treatments for diverse pathogens.</p></p>
