<p><ul><li>Non-hormonal</li><li>Hormonal</li><li>Inert</li></ul></p>
<p><p>Intrauterine Contraceptive Devices (IUCDs) are vital for family planning, classified into three main types. Non-hormonal IUCDs, such as copper devices, prevent fertilization by creating an inflammatory response in the uterus. Hormonal IUCDs release progestin, thickening cervical mucus and thinning the uterine lining to inhibit pregnancy. Inert IUCDs are designed solely for physical presence without any medication, primarily serving as a barrier to sperm. Each type offers different benefits, catering to diverse individual needs and preferences in contraception.</p></p>
