<p><p>The digital radiography market encompasses two primary types: Computed Radiography (CR) and Digital Radiography (DR). CR technology utilizes photo-stimulable phosphor plates to capture images, which are then converted into digital format using a scanner. This method is often cost-effective but slower. In contrast, DR technology employs digital detectors that capture and transmit images instantly, offering superior image quality and efficiency. Both systems enhance diagnostic capabilities in medical settings, catering to different operational needs and budgets.</p></p>
